Sacheon Traditional Memory Game

A neighborhood board game for laughing, remembering, and connecting

Players: 2–6 Play time: 10–20 min Difficulty: Easy–Medium

1. Overview

The Sacheon Traditional Memory Game features 24 pairs of cards inspired by the sights and culture of Sacheon. Players flip cards and find matching pairs. Beyond memory training, it is a social game for neighbors to talk about local nature, foods, and festivals together.

2. Components

  • 48 picture cards (24 pairs) — Namildae Beach, Sacheon Sea Cable Car, Mt. Waryong, and more
  • Card backs — traditional dancheong pattern
  • Rulebook (this page)

3. Setup

  1. Thoroughly shuffle all cards.
  2. Lay them face down in a neat rectangular grid.
  3. The player who most recently visited Sacheon goes first.

4. How to Play

  1. Flip two cards — On your turn, flip any two cards and check the pictures.
  2. If they match — Take the pair and play another turn.
  3. If they don’t — Turn both cards face down in their original spots. Play passes to the next player.
  4. End of game — When all pairs are found, the player with the most pairs wins.

5. Scoring (Optional)

ActionPoints
Match a pair+2
Two matches in a row+1 bonus
Mismatch0

6. Difficulty Options

  • Easy: use 8 pairs (16 cards)
  • Standard: use 16 pairs (32 cards)
  • Challenge: use all 24 pairs (48 cards)
Adjust the number of cards or offer a brief preview to make it accessible for everyone.

7. Community Variants

  • Story time: After a match, share a memory about the place/food on the cards.
  • Speed run: In 1 minute, match as many pairs as possible.
  • Co‑op mode: Work together to clear all pairs and record your time.

8. Cleanup & Storage

  1. Group cards by pairs and pack them neatly.
  2. Avoid moisture and direct sunlight; store in a dry place.
Note: Handle gently to avoid bending corners or paper cuts.
